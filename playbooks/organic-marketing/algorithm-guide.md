# Platform Algorithm Guide

> How the major platforms decide who sees your content — and how to work with each algorithm instead of against it.

---

## Instagram Algorithm (2025-2026)

### How It Works
Instagram uses multiple algorithms — one for Feed, one for Stories, one for Reels, and one for Explore. They all prioritize **relationship signals** (do you interact with this person?) and **content quality signals** (does this post keep people engaged?).

### Ranking Factors (In Order of Importance)
1. **Relationship**: How often the viewer interacts with your account (DMs, comments, likes, profile visits)
2. **Interest**: Does the content match what the viewer typically engages with?
3. **Timeliness**: Newer posts rank higher. The first 30-60 minutes of engagement are critical.
4. **Session behavior**: What has the viewer been engaging with in this session?

### Content Format Reach (Highest to Lowest)
1. **Reels** — 2-3x the reach of other formats. Instagram is competing with TikTok and actively pushes Reels to non-followers.
2. **Carousels** — Highest save rate and second-best reach. Each swipe counts as additional engagement.
3. **Single images** — Lowest organic reach but still valuable for portfolio and grid aesthetics.
4. **Stories** — Only shown to existing followers, but critical for deepening relationships and staying top of mind.

### Best Practices
- **First 30 minutes matter most.** Engage heavily on the platform before and after posting. Reply to every comment immediately.
- **Share your feed post to Stories** within 5 minutes of posting. Add a "New Post" sticker or "Tap to see" prompt.
- **Use all features.** The algorithm rewards accounts that use polls, questions, quizzes, music, location tags, mentions, and hashtags.
- **Consistent posting schedule.** The algorithm learns when your audience expects content. Post at the same times daily.
- **Reels sweet spot is 7-15 seconds.** Short enough to get replayed (replays count as views), long enough to deliver value.
- **Carousels: make them swipe.** The first slide is the hook, the last slide is the CTA. Instagram re-shows carousels to people who did not swipe the first time.
- **Collab posts** instantly double your reach by showing the post to both accounts' audiences.
- **Saves and shares are weighted more than likes.** Create content people want to reference later or send to a friend.

### Posting Times
- General best: 5-7 PM local time on weekdays, 10 AM-12 PM on weekends
- **Always check your own Instagram Insights** > Audience > Most Active Times. Your audience may differ.

### What Hurts You
- Deleting and reposting (resets engagement signals)
- Using banned or broken hashtags
- Engagement pods or fake engagement (Instagram detects and suppresses)
- Posting and immediately leaving the app
- Inconsistent posting (going silent for days then posting 5x in one day)

## TikTok Algorithm (2025-2026)

### How It Works
TikTok's algorithm is content-based, not follower-based. Every video is shown to a small test audience first. If that group engages, it gets pushed to a larger group, and so on. A brand new account with 0 followers can go viral on their first post.

### Ranking Factors (In Order of Importance)
1. **Watch time / completion rate** — The single most important factor. If people watch your whole video (or rewatch it), TikTok pushes it hard.
2. **Engagement rate** — Likes, comments, shares, saves relative to views.
3. **Trending sounds** — Using a trending sound can boost initial distribution by 40%+.
4. **Video information** — Captions, hashtags, and text overlays help TikTok categorize your content.

### Best Practices
- **Hook in the first 1 second.** You have one second before they scroll. Start with movement, a bold statement, or text overlay.
- **Use trending sounds** even if they are quiet in the background. The sound association boosts distribution.
- **Keep videos short.** 7-15 seconds is the sweet spot for completion rate. If your content needs more time, aim for under 60 seconds.
- **Reply to comments with video.** This creates a content loop, boosts engagement on the original video, and gives you endless content ideas.
- **Post 1-3x daily.** TikTok rewards volume more than any other platform. More posts = more chances to hit.
- **Use text overlays.** Most people scroll with sound off initially. Your hook needs to work visually.
- **Stitch and Duet** trending content in your niche. This piggybacks on existing momentum.

### Posting Times
- General best: 6-9 PM local time
- Secondary: 11 AM-1 PM (lunch break scrolling)
- **Check TikTok Analytics** > Followers > Most Active Times for your specific audience.

### What Hurts You
- Watermarks from other platforms (Instagram logo on a reposted Reel)
- Low resolution or blurry video
- Overly polished/commercial-looking content (TikTok favors raw and authentic)
- Long intros before getting to the point
- Not using any sound or music

## Facebook Algorithm (2025-2026)

### How It Works
Facebook prioritizes "meaningful interactions" — content that sparks conversations between people. It heavily favors content from friends and family over business pages.

### Key Insights for Beauty Pros
- **Personal profile reaches 25-50% more people** than a business page. Post your best content from your personal profile, then share it to your business page.
- **Groups are the goldmine.** Facebook Groups get significantly more organic reach than pages. Join 5-10 local community groups and provide value.
- **Comment threads boost visibility.** A post with a 20-comment thread will outperform a post with 100 likes and no comments. Ask questions.
- **Facebook Lives** still get preferential treatment in the algorithm, especially in Groups.
- **Shares are the most powerful signal.** Content that gets shared to someone's timeline or in a DM gets massive reach.

### Best Practices
- Post from your personal profile first, then share to your business page
- Join local groups: neighborhood groups, mom groups, women's groups, buy/sell/trade groups
- Provide value in groups WITHOUT being salesy. Answer questions. Share tips. When people check your profile, they will see what you do.
- Use Facebook Stories (very low competition, high visibility)
- Ask engaging questions that start conversations in the comments
- Go Live in groups (with admin permission) for Q&A sessions or demonstrations

### Posting Times
- General best: 9-11 AM on weekdays
- Secondary: 1-3 PM on weekdays
- Facebook audiences tend to skew slightly older — morning and midday perform better than evening

### What Hurts You
- Links in posts (Facebook suppresses external links to keep people on the platform)
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree, share if you love it")
- Posting only promotional content from a business page
- Ignoring comments and messages

## General Algorithm Principles (All Platforms)

### The Golden Rules

1. **Post when YOUR audience is online.** Check your analytics on each platform. Do not guess. Do not blindly follow generic "best times to post" advice.

2. **Engagement begets engagement.** The more you engage with others, the more the algorithm shows your content. Spend 15-20 minutes engaging before and after you post.

3. **Consistency beats virality.** One viral post followed by a week of silence does nothing for your business. A consistent daily poster with moderate engagement will outperform a sporadic poster with occasional spikes.

4. **The algorithm follows the money.** Platforms want users to stay on the platform. Content that keeps people watching, reading, commenting, and scrolling gets rewarded. Content that pushes people off the platform (external links, "link in bio" every post) gets suppressed.

5. **Early engagement is critical.** The first 30-60 minutes after posting determine how far your content reaches. Be available to respond to comments, reply to DMs, and engage with others during this window.

6. **Save-worthy and share-worthy content wins.** Create content people want to bookmark for later or send to a friend. Educational tips, transformation reveals, and relatable humor all drive saves and shares.

7. **Video dominates.** Across all platforms, video content (especially short-form vertical video) gets the most reach. If you are only posting photos, you are leaving reach on the table.
