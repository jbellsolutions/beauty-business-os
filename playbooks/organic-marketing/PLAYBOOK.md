# Organic Marketing Playbook

> Adapted from the "Organic Engine 2.0" framework (Real Beauty Bosses). Originally Facebook-focused — updated here for Instagram/TikTok-first while preserving the core methodology.

---

## The Hunt + Harvest Framework

Every day in your business has two modes:

- **Hunt** — Starting new conversations. This is lead generation. You are putting yourself in front of people who have never heard of you and making a memorable first impression.
- **Harvest** — Booking appointments from existing conversations. This is lead nurture. You are moving warm contacts toward a paid booking.

Most beauty pros spend all their time harvesting a tiny garden they never planted. The magic is in hunting *first*, then harvesting what you planted yesterday, last week, last month.

**The ratio**: Spend 60% of your marketing time hunting (new eyeballs, new conversations) and 40% harvesting (following up, nurturing, booking).

## Daily Activities — The Daily Hunt

These are non-negotiable daily actions. They take 60-90 minutes total. Do them before you see your first client or right after your last one.

### 1. Post Content Daily

- **Minimum**: 1 feed post + 2-3 stories
- **Ideal**: 1 feed post + 1 Reel + 5-7 stories throughout the day
- Feed posts build your grid (your portfolio). Stories build your personality and trust.
- Never post and ghost. Stay on the platform for 15-20 minutes after posting to engage.

### 2. Engage on 10+ Posts in Your Niche

- Find accounts your ideal clients follow (local influencers, complementary businesses, community pages).
- Leave **thoughtful** comments. Not "love this!" or a single emoji. Write 2-3 sentences that add value or start a conversation.
- Examples: "This color is stunning on you! Did you go warmer this season?" or "I always recommend this product to my clients too — the results are unreal."
- This puts your name and profile in front of potential clients organically.

### 3. Add/Follow 20-40 New Targeted Accounts Daily

- These are potential clients in your service area.
- Find them through: local hashtags, location tags at nearby businesses, followers of complementary local accounts (gyms, salons, boutiques), engagement on local influencer posts.
- Follow, then engage on 2-3 of their recent posts before you DM them.

### 4. Start 10-30+ DM Conversations Daily

- These are warm intros, not sales pitches.
- Sources for DMs: new followers (thank them), people who engaged on your content (thank them), people whose content you genuinely liked, story replies.
- The goal is a *conversation*, not a booking. Bookings come from conversations naturally.

### 5. Reply to EVERY Comment Within 1 Hour

- This is critical for the algorithm. Platforms reward posts that generate back-and-forth conversation.
- Reply with a question to keep the thread going. The longer the comment thread, the more the platform pushes your post.
- Pin the best comments to the top.

## Weekly Activities

### Monday — Review + Plan
- Check analytics from last week. What got the most reach? Most saves? Most DMs? Double down on winners.
- Plan this week's content themes.

### Content Day (Sunday or your day off)
- Batch film 5-7 pieces of content.
- Film in different outfits/locations so it does not all look like the same day.
- Get B-roll: your workspace, products, hands working, before/after angles.

### Midweek — Model Call or Special Offer Post
- Every 1-2 weeks, post a model call or limited-time offer.
- This creates urgency and fills slow days on your calendar.

### Weekly Content Targets
- 1 educational carousel or Reel (positions you as the expert)
- 1 transformation post (before/after or client reaction)
- 1 personal/personality post (builds trust and relatability)
- 1 testimonial or social proof post
- 1 promotional post (offer, availability, booking CTA)

## The Conversation Framework

Every DM follows this path:

```
Opener (warm, personal)
  → Build rapport (2-3 exchanges about THEM)
    → Identify need (ask about their goals/struggles)
      → Soft pitch (mention how you help with exactly that)
        → Book (send your booking link)
```

### Script: New Follower DM

```
Hey [name]! Thanks so much for the follow — I love your page!
I noticed you're in [city] too. Are you local? I'm always excited
to connect with people in the area.
```

Wait for reply. Build rapport. Then:

```
That's awesome! So what I do is [brief description of your service].
If you've ever been curious about [specific result], I'd love to chat
more about it. No pressure at all!
```

### Script: Comment-to-DM

When someone comments on your post:

```
Hey [name]! I saw your comment on my [topic] post — thank you!
I'm curious, have you ever tried [service related to post topic]?
```

### Script: Story Reply

When someone replies to your story:

```
Ahh thank you so much! That means a lot.
Are you into [related topic]? I'd love to know what you're
currently doing for [skin/hair/brows/etc].
```

### Script: Cold DM to Local Prospect

```
Hey [name]! I came across your profile and love your aesthetic.
I'm a [your title] here in [city] and I just wanted to say hi
and connect with more amazing women in the area!
```

**Rules for all DMs:**
- Never lead with your service or a pitch
- Never send a booking link unprompted
- Ask more questions than you answer
- Use voice notes when possible (builds trust 10x faster)
- If they are not interested, be gracious and move on

## Low Hanging Fruit — The Warm List

Every week, build a list of 50-100 warm contacts. These are people who:

- Liked or commented on your posts this week
- Viewed your stories multiple times
- Sent you a DM (even just an emoji reaction)
- You had a conversation with but they did not book yet
- Past clients who have not rebooked

**Work the list.** Follow up 3x per week with each warm lead. Not pushy — just staying top of mind:

- Monday: React to their story or comment on their post
- Wednesday: Send a voice note or personal message
- Friday: Share something relevant (a tip, a before/after, an opening in your calendar)

The fortune is in the follow-up. Most people need 5-7 touchpoints before they book.

## Model Call Strategy

A model call is a discounted or free service offered in exchange for content and reviews.

### How to Run One

1. **Post the offer**: "Looking for 2-3 models this week for [service] at a special rate of $[discounted price]. You'll get the full treatment — I just ask that I can take before/after photos and a short video for my portfolio. DM me MODEL to grab a spot!"
2. **Select carefully**: Choose people who match your ideal client demographic and have engaged followings.
3. **Deliver exceptional service**: This is your audition. Go above and beyond.
4. **Capture content**: Before photo, process shots, after photo, client reaction video, video testimonial, written review.
5. **Convert to full-price client**: At checkout say "I'd love to keep working with you! Your next appointment at regular pricing would be $[price]. Want me to get you on the calendar for [3-4 weeks out]?"

### Model Call Frequency
- When building your book: 2-3 model calls per week
- Once established: 1-2 per month (to keep fresh content flowing)
- Always have a model call running when launching a new service

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Instagram
- **Reels get 2x+ the reach** of static posts. Prioritize short-form video.
- Use 20-30 hashtags (mix of large, medium, small, micro). Put them in the caption, not the comments.
- Post between 5-7 PM local time (or whenever your insights say your audience is most active).
- Share every feed post to your stories with a "tap to see" sticker.
- Use every feature: polls, questions, quizzes, countdowns, music, location tags.
- Carousels get the highest save rate — great for educational content.
- Collab posts with complementary businesses double your reach instantly.

### TikTok
- **Hook in the first 1 second.** If you do not grab attention immediately, they scroll.
- Use trending sounds — they boost reach by 40%+ even if the sound is barely audible.
- Post 1-3x daily. TikTok rewards volume more than any other platform.
- Post between 6-9 PM (or check your analytics).
- Reply to comments with video — this creates a content loop and boosts engagement.
- Fewer hashtags (3-5 specific ones). Use trending hashtags when relevant.
- Raw and authentic outperforms polished and produced.

### Facebook
- **Groups are the goldmine.** Join 5-10 local community groups. Provide value. Do not spam your services.
- Your personal profile reaches 25-50% more people than a business page. Post on your personal profile and share to your business page.
- Facebook Lives still get strong reach in groups.
- Comment threads boost visibility — ask questions that spark discussion.
- Marketplace can work for posting service specials.

### General Rules (All Platforms)
- Post when YOUR audience is online. Check your analytics — do not guess.
- Engagement begets engagement. The more you give, the more you get.
- Consistency beats virality. One viral post means nothing without consistent follow-up.
- Repurpose everything. One video becomes a Reel, a TikTok, a Story, a carousel, and a quote graphic.
- Save trending audios even when you do not have an idea yet. You will use them later.
