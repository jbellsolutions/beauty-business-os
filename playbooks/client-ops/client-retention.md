# Client Retention Strategies

> Acquiring a new client costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one. A client who rebooks 6+ times is worth $1,000-$5,000+ in lifetime value. Retention is the highest-leverage activity in your business.

---

## Strategy 1: Rebook at Checkout

This is the single most impactful retention tactic. Before your client leaves, book their next appointment.

### How to Ask
Do not ask "Do you want to rebook?" — this makes it too easy to say no.

Instead, assume the rebook:
- "Let's get your next one on the calendar. I'd recommend [X weeks] from now — does [specific date] work for you?"
- "To maintain these results, you'll want to come back in [timeframe]. I have a [day] at [time] open — want me to hold that for you?"
- "I'm going to get you scheduled for your next appointment so we can keep this momentum going."

### Why It Works
- They are at peak satisfaction (they just got a great service)
- It is easier to say yes in the moment than to remember to book later
- Their calendar is in front of them
- You have their full attention

### Target
- Rebook rate goal: **80%+** of clients should leave with their next appointment on the calendar

## Strategy 2: Birthday Messages + Special Offers

Everyone loves to feel remembered on their birthday.

### Setup (via GHL or CRM)
1. Collect birthday during intake form (just month and day)
2. Set up an automated message 1 week before their birthday:

```
Happy almost-birthday, [name]! 🎂

I wanted to do something special for you — here's [X% off / a free
add-on / a birthday treat] on your next visit. Just book by [date]
to use it!

You deserve to be pampered. Book here: [BOOKING LINK]
```

3. Send a "Happy Birthday" message on the actual day (personal text, not automated, if possible)

## Strategy 3: "We Miss You" Campaigns

When a regular client goes quiet, reach out before they forget about you.

### Trigger Points

**30 Days Since Last Visit:**
```
Hey [name]! It's been a little while — just thinking about you
and wondering how your [skin/lashes/hair/etc] is doing!

When you're ready to come back in, I'd love to see you.
Book here: [BOOKING LINK]
```

**60 Days Since Last Visit:**
```
Hey [name]! I noticed it's been about 2 months since your last
visit. I miss your face!

I've got some new [services/products/techniques] I think you'd
love. Want to come in and check them out? Here's a little
something to sweeten the deal: [SPECIAL OFFER — e.g., $15 off
your next visit]

Book here: [BOOKING LINK]
```

**90 Days Since Last Visit:**
```
Hey [name]! It's been a while and I just wanted you to know
my door is always open!

If anything wasn't perfect about your last visit, I'd love the
chance to make it right. And if life just got busy (I get it!),
I'd love to welcome you back with [GENEROUS OFFER — e.g., 20% off].

No pressure at all. Just wanted you to know I'm here!

[BOOKING LINK]
```

### Automation Setup
- Tag clients by last visit date in your CRM
- Set up automated triggers at 30/60/90 days since last appointment
- Personalize as much as possible (reference their specific service, their name, anything memorable)

## Strategy 4: Loyalty Program

Reward repeat clients for their continued business.

### Simple Points System
- Every visit earns 1 point
- 5 points = free add-on service (e.g., lip treatment with facial, hand massage with manicure)
- 10 points = $[X] credit toward any service
- Track with a simple punch card (physical or digital in your CRM)

### How to Introduce It
At the end of their appointment:
```
"By the way, I started a loyalty program! Every time you come in,
you earn a point. After 5 visits, you get a free [add-on]. You
already have 1 point!"
```

### Keep It Simple
- Do not overcomplicate with tiers, multipliers, or expiration dates
- The easier it is to understand, the more effective it is
- Announce their point status at every visit: "You're at 3 points — only 2 more until your free [add-on]!"

## Strategy 5: VIP Membership Program

Monthly memberships create predictable recurring revenue and lock in retention.

### Structure Example
```
VIP Membership — $[X]/month

Includes:
→ 1 [primary service] per month (valued at $[higher price])
→ [X]% off all additional services
→ [X]% off retail products
→ Priority booking (members get first access to new time slots)
→ 1 free add-on per quarter
→ Birthday month: 1 complimentary [service]
```

### Pricing Strategy
- Price the membership at 15-25% less than the a la carte price
- The client saves money. You gain guaranteed monthly revenue and a committed client.
- Example: If your facial is $120, offer membership at $95/month

### How to Pitch
```
"I wanted to tell you about my VIP membership — it's basically the
best deal I offer. You get your monthly [service] at a discount,
plus [perks]. Most of my regulars are on it because it saves them
about $[X] per year. Want me to get you set up?"
```

### Management
- Use a payment processor (Stripe, Square) for recurring billing
- Track membership status in your CRM
- Send a monthly "Your membership service is ready to book!" reminder

## Strategy 6: Referral Incentive Program

Turn your best clients into your best marketers.

### Structure
- **Referrer gets:** $[X] off their next service (or a free add-on)
- **Referred friend gets:** $[X] off their first visit
- Both parties benefit — this makes it a genuine recommendation, not a sales pitch

### How to Ask for Referrals
After a great appointment:
```
"If you have any friends who've been looking for a [your service],
I'd love to take care of them! When they book and mention your name,
you both get $[X] off. I'll make sure to take extra good care of them."
```

### Referral Cards (Physical or Digital)
Create a simple card or shareable graphic:
```
You've been referred by [NAME]!

Book your first [service] and get $[X] off.
Your friend gets $[X] off too!

Book here: [BOOKING LINK]
Mention "[REFERRER NAME]" when you book.
```

### Tracking
- Track referrals in your CRM (tag the referrer and the new client)
- Thank the referrer every time: "Hey [name], your friend [new client] came in today! She was amazing. Your $[X] credit has been applied to your account!"

## Strategy 7: Personal Touches

The small things create loyalty that no discount can match.

### Remember Details
- Their preferred pressure/temperature/style
- Names of their kids, partner, pets
- Their job, hobbies, recent events they mentioned
- Their coffee or drink order
- Note these in your CRM after every appointment

### Use What You Remember
- "How did your daughter's recital go?"
- "I remembered you like it a little cooler in here — I adjusted the temperature for you."
- "I saw that coffee shop you mentioned and thought of you!"

### Surprise and Delight
- Unexpected free sample or mini product at checkout
- Handwritten thank-you card after their first visit (or after a milestone visit)
- A small holiday gift for your top 10 clients (branded candle, mini product set)
- Text them when you get a new product you know they will love

## Strategy 8: Seasonal Check-Ins

Reach out with relevant, timely messages tied to seasons or events.

### Examples by Niche

**Esthetician:**
- Spring: "Time to prep your skin for summer! Let's talk SPF and hydration."
- Fall: "Summer is over — let's repair any sun damage and get your fall routine set."
- Winter: "Dry skin season is here. Your skin needs extra moisture — let's get you in."

**Lash Tech:**
- Before holidays: "Holiday bookings are filling up fast! Get your lashes done before the rush."
- Summer: "Waterproof lash extensions = pool-proof confidence all summer."

**Hair Stylist:**
- Every 6-8 weeks: "Your color is probably ready for a refresh! Want to get on the calendar?"
- Before school: "Back-to-school haircuts are booking now!"

**Nail Tech:**
- Before holidays/events: "Holiday nails! What design are you thinking this year?"
- Summer: "Toe season is here! Let's get those pedicures booked."

## Retention Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | How to Calculate |
|---|---|---|
| Rebook rate at checkout | 80%+ | Clients who rebook at checkout / total clients seen |
| Client return rate (within 90 days) | 70%+ | Clients who return within 90 days / total clients |
| Average client lifetime | 12+ months | Average time from first to last appointment |
| Average visits per client per year | 8-12 | Total appointments / unique clients |
| Referral rate | 15-20% | New clients from referrals / total new clients |
| Membership retention (monthly) | 90%+ | Active members / members at start of month |

Review these monthly. If any metric is dropping, investigate and adjust your strategy.
