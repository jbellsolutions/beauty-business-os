# Appointment Booking Flow

> Every lead follows this path: Inquiry → Qualify → Book → Confirm → Remind → Serve → Follow Up. This document defines every step.

---

## Lead Sources

Leads come from five main channels:

| Source | Typical Message | Response Time Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram DM | "How much is [service]?" | Under 15 minutes (ManyChat instant, human follow-up within 15 min) |
| Website form | Contact or booking form submission | Under 30 minutes |
| Referral | "My friend [name] told me about you" | Under 30 minutes |
| Walk-in | Shows up in person | Immediate |
| Phone/Text | Call or SMS inquiry | Under 15 minutes |

**Speed matters.** Responding within 5 minutes makes you 10x more likely to book the lead than responding in 30 minutes.

---

## Step 1: Qualify the Lead

Before booking, gather the essential information:

### Questions to Ask
1. **What service are you interested in?** (Determines appointment length, pricing, and prep instructions)
2. **Have you had this service before?** (First-timers need extra time and education)
3. **Any contraindications?** (Allergies, medications, skin conditions, pregnancy — depends on your service)
4. **What's your availability?** (Narrow down to 2-3 specific options)

### Qualification Script (DM/Text)
```
Hey [name]! Thanks for reaching out — I'd love to help!

Quick questions so I can get you booked with the right service:

1. What service were you thinking? (Here's my menu: [link])
2. Have you had this done before?
3. Any skin sensitivities or allergies I should know about?

Once I know that, I'll send you a few time options!
```

### Red Flags (Do Not Book)
- Cannot answer basic questions about what they want
- Only focused on getting the cheapest possible price
- Rude or disrespectful in initial messages
- Wants you to do something outside your scope or training

## Step 2: Book the Appointment

### Option A: GHL Calendar Widget (Preferred)
1. Send the client your online booking link
2. They select their service, preferred date/time, and enter their info
3. System auto-confirms and adds to your calendar
4. Payment (deposit or full) collected at booking if applicable

### Option B: Manual Booking
1. Offer 2-3 specific time slots: "I have openings Tuesday at 2 PM, Wednesday at 10 AM, or Thursday at 4 PM — which works best?"
2. Never say "when works for you?" — giving options makes it easier to commit
3. Once they choose, confirm all details:
   - Service
   - Date and time
   - Duration
   - Price
   - Location/address
   - Any prep instructions
4. Add to your calendar manually
5. Send confirmation message

### Booking Confirmation Message
```
You're all set! Here are your appointment details:

Service: [SERVICE NAME]
Date: [DAY, DATE]
Time: [TIME]
Duration: [DURATION]
Price: [PRICE]
Location: [ADDRESS]

Before your appointment:
[PREP INSTRUCTIONS — e.g., "Come with a clean face, no makeup"]

If you need to reschedule, please let me know at least 24 hours
in advance.

I can't wait to see you! Let me know if you have any questions.
```

---

## Step 3: Pre-Appointment Sequence

### Immediately After Booking
- Send confirmation message (above)
- Send intake form (if applicable — medical history, consent, skin questionnaire)
- Send "what to expect" info for first-time clients

### 24 Hours Before
```
Hey [name]! Just a friendly reminder about your [SERVICE] appointment
tomorrow at [TIME].

Here's the address: [ADDRESS]
Parking: [PARKING INSTRUCTIONS]

Quick reminders:
→ [Prep instruction 1]
→ [Prep instruction 2]
→ Please arrive 5-10 minutes early

See you tomorrow!
```

### 2 Hours Before
```
Hey [name]! Looking forward to seeing you at [TIME] today!
Here's the address one more time: [ADDRESS]

See you soon!
```

## Step 4: Post-Appointment Sequence

### Immediately After (At Checkout)
- Discuss results and aftercare
- **Rebook their next appointment** (this is the single most important retention action)
- Ask: "Want to go ahead and get your next one on the calendar? I'd recommend [timeframe] from now."
- Collect payment
- Provide printed or digital aftercare instructions

### 2-4 Hours After
```
Hey [name]! I hope you're loving your [SERVICE]!

Here's a quick recap of your aftercare:
→ [Aftercare instruction 1]
→ [Aftercare instruction 2]
→ [Aftercare instruction 3]

Any questions at all, don't hesitate to reach out!
```

### 24 Hours After
```
Hey [name]! How's everything looking/feeling today?

If you have a minute, I would absolutely love a review — it means
the world to my small business!

[GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

Thank you so much — I loved working with you!
```

### 1 Week After
```
Hey [name]! Just checking in — how's your [SERVICE] holding up?

If you haven't already, let's get your next appointment on the
calendar! I recommend coming back in [TIMEFRAME] for best results.

Book here: [BOOKING LINK]
```

## No-Show Protocol

No-shows cost you time and money. Have a clear policy and enforce it gently.

### When a Client Does Not Show Up

**At appointment time:** Wait 15 minutes.

**15 minutes past:** Call the client.

**If no answer (within 30 minutes):**
```
Hey [name]! I had you down for [TIME] today and wanted to make
sure everything is okay. I hope nothing happened!

If something came up, no worries — let's get you rescheduled.
Here's my calendar: [BOOKING LINK]
```

**If no response within 24 hours:**
```
Hey [name]! I missed you yesterday at your [SERVICE] appointment.
I totally understand things come up!

I'd love to get you rescheduled. I have openings on [DATE] and
[DATE] — want to grab one of those?
```

### Prevention
- Require a deposit at booking (even $20 reduces no-shows dramatically)
- Send reminders at 24 hours and 2 hours before
- Have a clear cancellation policy: "Please cancel or reschedule at least 24 hours in advance. Late cancellations or no-shows may be charged a $[X] fee."
- State the policy at booking and in confirmation messages

### Repeat No-Shows
- First no-show: Gentle reschedule message (above)
- Second no-show: Require full pre-payment for next booking
- Third no-show: Politely decline future bookings

## Cancellation / Reschedule Flow

### Client Requests to Cancel (24+ hours notice)
```
No problem at all! I've cancelled your appointment for [DATE].

When you're ready to rebook, here's my calendar: [BOOKING LINK]

Hope to see you soon!
```

### Client Requests to Cancel (Less than 24 hours)
```
I totally understand things come up! Just a heads up — my
cancellation policy is 24 hours notice.

This time I'll waive the fee, but for future reference, please
try to give me at least 24 hours notice so I can fill the spot.

Want to reschedule? I have openings on [DATE] and [DATE].
```

### Client Wants to Reschedule
```
Of course! Let's get you moved. I have these openings:

→ [Option 1]
→ [Option 2]
→ [Option 3]

Which works best?
```

## Walk-In Protocol

If someone walks in without an appointment:

1. **Greet warmly**: "Hey! Welcome! Do you have an appointment, or are you just checking things out?"
2. **If you have availability**: Book them on the spot. Treat them like a VIP for walking in.
3. **If you are fully booked**: "I'm so sorry, I'm fully booked today! But I would love to get you on the calendar. What day works best for you?" Book them before they leave.
4. **Always**: Give them a card, point them to your Instagram, and ask if they'd like to be added to your contact list for specials.
