# Review Collection System

> Reviews are the #1 trust signal for new clients deciding whether to book with you. A steady stream of recent, positive reviews is worth more than any ad campaign.

---

## The Goal

Ask every single client for a review. Every one. No exceptions.

- **20-30% will leave a review** when asked. Most people are happy to — they just need the nudge.
- At 5 clients per day, that is 1-2 reviews per day.
- That is **30-60 new reviews per month.**
- Within 6 months, you will have more reviews than 95% of your competitors.

---

## Timing

Send the review request **24 hours after the appointment** — not immediately at checkout.

Why 24 hours:
- They have had time to look in the mirror, get compliments, and feel the results
- They are still in the "afterglow" of the experience
- It does not feel transactional (like you only care about the review, not them)

---

## Channel Priority

1. **SMS / Text message** — 98% open rate, highest conversion
2. **Instagram DM** — if that is how you primarily communicate with them
3. **Email** — lowest conversion but still worth it as a backup

Always default to the channel you normally communicate on with that client.

---

## The Review Request Script

### Primary Message (24 hours after appointment)

**Via Text/SMS:**
```
Hey [name]! I hope you're still loving your [service] from
yesterday! You looked amazing.

If you have 30 seconds, I would be SO grateful for a quick Google
review. It's the biggest way you can support my small business
and it helps other people find me!

[GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

Thank you so much — you're the best!
```

**Via Instagram DM:**
```
Hey [name]! How's everything looking today? I loved working
with you yesterday!

If you get a chance, would you mind dropping a quick review?
It literally takes 30 seconds and it means the world to me.

[GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

Thank you!!
```

### Key Elements of the Script
- **Personalize**: Use their name and reference their specific service
- **Make it easy**: Include the direct link (not "go to Google and search for my business")
- **Make it quick**: Emphasize it only takes 30 seconds
- **Express gratitude**: Thank them before they even do it
- **Explain why**: "It helps other people find me" — people want to help when they know the impact

---

## Follow-Up (If No Review in 3 Days)

If they have not left a review after 3 days, send ONE gentle follow-up:

```
Hey [name]! No worries at all if you haven't had a chance — I know
life gets busy!

If you do get a minute, that review link is here whenever you're
ready: [GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]

Either way, I can't wait to see you at your next appointment!
```

**Rules:**
- Only follow up once. Do not nag.
- Keep the tone light and no-pressure.
- If they do not leave a review after the follow-up, let it go and focus on the next client.

## Where to Send Reviews

### Priority: Google Business Profile
- Google reviews show up in search results and Google Maps
- They are the most visible and trusted review platform
- If you do not have a Google Business Profile, set one up immediately

### How to Get Your Google Review Link
1. Go to your Google Business Profile
2. Click "Ask for reviews" or go to your profile and click "Get more reviews"
3. Copy the short link provided
4. Save this link everywhere — in your phone, in your CRM, as a saved reply

### Secondary: Yelp
- Important in some markets, less so in others
- Do NOT ask for Yelp reviews directly (Yelp penalizes businesses that solicit reviews)
- Instead, make sure your Yelp profile is claimed and complete. Organic reviews will come.

### Tertiary: Facebook Page
- Helpful for social proof but less impactful than Google
- Use for clients who are not on Google but are active on Facebook

## Automation Setup

### In GHL (GoHighLevel) or Your CRM
1. Create an automation trigger: "Appointment completed"
2. Add a 24-hour delay
3. Send SMS with review request script (personalized with client name and service)
4. Add a condition: If no review received in 3 days, send follow-up message
5. Tag the client as "Review Requested" so you do not ask again for the same appointment

### Manual Process (If No CRM)
- Every evening, text review requests to clients you saw yesterday
- Keep a simple spreadsheet: Client name | Date of service | Review requested (Y/N) | Review received (Y/N)

## Responding to Reviews

### Positive Reviews (Respond within 24 hours)
```
Thank you so much, [name]! You are the sweetest. I absolutely
loved working with you and I can't wait to see you again!
[Optional: reference something specific about their visit]
```

Keep it personal. Do not use the same copy-paste reply for every review — people notice.

### Negative Reviews (Respond within 2 hours)

**This is urgent.** A negative review without a response looks 10x worse than one with a thoughtful reply.

**Response Template:**
```
Hi [name], thank you for taking the time to share your feedback.
I'm really sorry to hear that your experience didn't meet your
expectations — that's never what I want for my clients.

I'd love the opportunity to make this right. Would you mind
reaching out to me directly at [phone/email] so we can chat?
I want to understand what happened and find a solution.

Thank you again, and I hope to hear from you.
```

**Rules for Negative Review Responses:**
- Never be defensive or argumentative
- Never blame the client publicly
- Always take it offline (offer to discuss via phone, text, or in person)
- Empathize first, then offer a resolution
- Other potential clients are reading your response — they want to see that you handle problems gracefully

### Making It Right
When you connect with the unhappy client:
1. Listen fully. Let them explain without interrupting.
2. Apologize sincerely, even if you think they are wrong.
3. Offer a specific resolution: redo the service for free, partial refund, or discount on next visit.
4. If they accept and are satisfied, politely ask if they would consider updating their review.
5. If they refuse to engage, let it go. Your professional public response speaks for itself.

## Turning Reviews Into Content

Positive reviews are not just trust signals — they are free marketing content.

### How to Repurpose Reviews

1. **Screenshot + Story Post**: Screenshot the review, post it to your Instagram Story with a "Thank you [name]!" overlay
2. **Quote Graphic**: Turn the best line from the review into a branded graphic for your feed
3. **Carousel**: Compile 5-10 review quotes into a carousel post: "What my clients are saying"
4. **Video**: Film yourself reading a touching review (great for Reels/TikTok)
5. **Website**: Add a rotating review section to your website or booking page
6. **Highlight Reel**: Create an Instagram Highlight called "Reviews" and save all review stories there

### Posting Schedule for Reviews
- Share 1-2 review screenshots in Stories per week
- Post 1 review-focused feed post per month (carousel or quote graphic)
- Update your website reviews quarterly

## Review Collection Metrics

| Metric | Target | How to Calculate |
|---|---|---|
| Ask rate | 100% | Reviews requested / total clients served |
| Review conversion rate | 20-30% | Reviews received / reviews requested |
| Reviews per month | 30-60 | Total new reviews in a calendar month |
| Average star rating | 4.8+ | Average of all review scores |
| Response time (positive) | Under 24 hours | Time from review posted to your response |
| Response time (negative) | Under 2 hours | Time from review posted to your response |
| Google review total (6 month goal) | 100+ | Cumulative Google reviews |

Track monthly. If your conversion rate drops below 20%, revisit your script — it may need freshening up.

## Quick Reference: Review Request Checklist

For every client, every appointment:

- [ ] Service completed and client is happy
- [ ] Rebooked their next appointment (retention first, review second)
- [ ] 24 hours later: Send review request via text with direct Google link
- [ ] 3 days later (if no review): Send one gentle follow-up
- [ ] Review received: Respond within 24 hours with a personalized thank you
- [ ] Share positive review as Story content
- [ ] Log in tracking sheet or CRM
