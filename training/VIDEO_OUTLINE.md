# Training Video Outline

> **Funnel context:** This walkthrough video is the post-download onboarding step in the sales funnel. After a beauty professional completes the Level 1 certification and downloads the OS, this is the next thing they watch. See `sales/FUNNEL.md` for the full funnel map.

**Length:** 12-15 minutes
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover (Justin records)
**Audience:** Non-technical beauty professionals who have never used a terminal or Claude Code

---

## Intro (0:00 - 0:45)

"Hey! Welcome to Beauty Business OS. This is your AI business manager — it handles your content, appointments, follow-ups, marketing, and reporting. You just talk to it. Let me show you how to set it up. It takes about 5 minutes."

## Part 1: Installing Claude Code (0:45 - 2:30)

**Show on screen:**
1. Go to claude.ai/download
2. Click Download for Mac (or Windows)
3. Open the installer, drag to Applications
4. Open Claude Code (show the app icon)

"That's it for installation. One download, one click."

## Part 2: Opening the Project (2:30 - 3:30)

**Show on screen:**
1. Open Claude Code desktop app
2. Open the beauty-business-os folder
3. Show the interface — the chat area where you type

"This is where you'll talk to your business. Think of this as your business partner's office — you walk in, ask questions, give instructions, and it handles everything."

## Part 3: The Setup — "Set Me Up" (3:30 - 7:00)

**Show on screen:**
1. Type "set me up" and hit enter
2. Walk through each question:
   - Business name
   - Niche (show the dropdown: lash tech, esthetician, etc.)
   - Services and pricing
   - Location
   - Brand voice (show the options: fun & girly, professional, etc.)
   - GoHighLevel connection (show pasting API key or skipping)
   - Metricool connection (show the free signup and API key)
   - Image generation (show Replicate signup)
3. Show the confirmation screen
4. Say "yes" to finalize

"It just configured your entire business system based on those answers. Your brand voice, your services, your pricing — everything is personalized."

## Part 4: Your First Commands (7:00 - 10:00)

**Show 4-5 commands in action:**

1. "What's on my schedule today?"
   - Show the morning briefing output

2. "Create this week's content"
   - Show it generating social posts with their brand voice
   - Point out the hashtags, hooks, CTAs

3. "Make me a promo graphic for [their top service]"
   - Show image generation (if Replicate connected)
   - Show the output image

4. "How much did I make this week?"
   - Show revenue summary

5. "What should I post today?"
   - Show content suggestion

"See? No menus. No buttons. No learning curve. You just talk to it like you'd talk to a business partner."

## Part 5: The Dashboard (10:00 - 11:00)

**Show on screen:**
1. Open dashboard/index.html in Chrome
2. Walk through each section: appointments, revenue, content, leads, tasks
3. "Your AI manager updates this throughout the day. Just refresh to see the latest."

## Part 6: Daily Workflow (11:00 - 12:30)

"Here's what your typical day looks like with Beauty Business OS:"

1. **Morning:** Open Claude Code. "What's on my plate today?" — get your briefing
2. **Before clients:** "Prep me for my 10am" — get client notes and upsell suggestions
3. **Between clients:** "Post that reel I filmed yesterday at 6pm" — schedule content
4. **End of day:** "How did I do today?" — get your daily summary
5. **Sunday:** "Plan my content for next week" — batch create all content

"That's it. Your AI handles the business side so you can focus on your clients."

## Outro (12:30 - 13:30)

"You're all set. If you ever get stuck, just ask 'help me with [whatever]' and it'll guide you. Check the docs folder for FAQs and troubleshooting. Welcome to the future of running your beauty business."

"And when you're ready for the next level — fully automated social media, content created and posted for you across all platforms — ask about Beauty Boss OS, the Boss Edition. That's the Boss Blowout. But for now, you've got everything you need. Go talk to your business."

## Recording Notes

- Use a clean, fresh setup for the demo (clear CLIENT_CONFIG.md before recording)
- Use "lash tech" as the demo niche (most visual, most relatable)
- Use "fun & girly" as the demo voice (most personality, shows the voice system clearly)
- Keep energy high but not salesy — this is a tutorial, not a pitch
- Pause briefly after each command to let the output load and be readable
- If something takes time to generate, cut/speed up the waiting
